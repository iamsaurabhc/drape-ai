Build a Batch AI Image Pipeline for Fashion Outfits (50–100 SKUs per run)
Posted 9 hours ago

Summary
The goal
We're building a scalable system that takes our product photos (jackets, shirts, trousers, in cotton, wool and knit etc.) and transforms them into professional fashion photos of an AI-generated model wearing complete outfits — at scale. Think 50-100 outfits per collection, fully styled and consistent, ready for e-commerce listings, social campaigns, and PDP pages.

We've already validated that AI can produce the quality we need on a per-outfit basis. The challenge now is scale — we need a workflow that lets us feed in a batch of outfits and get back a batch of finished images, without manual intervention between outfits.

What you'll be working on
You'll design and build the pipeline that connects the dots between:

Input — a structured list of outfits (each with its garment images and a model reference)

Generation — the AI image step that produces the model wearing each complete outfit on a consistent backdrop

Output — finished photos automatically delivered to a Google Drive folder, organized per outfit

The output should look authentic, premium, and brand-ready — natural fabric drape, accurate garment fit, consistent lighting, and the same model identity across every photo. We're aiming for fashion campaign quality, not basic AI mockups.

Who we're looking for
The ideal collaborator has experience in both AI image generation for fashion and workflow automation. You should be comfortable with advanced AI tools and understand how to chain them into a repeatable pipeline.

Equally important: you understand fashion aesthetics. You can tell the difference between an AI-generated photo that looks "almost right" and one that's commercially ready. Garment fit, fabric texture, closures, layering, shadows, model consistency — these matter.

You should also be comfortable with workflow automation tools — n8n, Make, ComfyUI, or custom code — to handle the batch logic, error retries, and output organization.

A working manual pipeline that produces good single-outfit output (currently in Magnific Spaces — but we're not committed to that platform if you propose something better) as the API is useless

A short handover loom link

Cost per finished image should be reasonable (under $0.50 in AI compute is a rough target)
Wall-clock for a 50-outfit batch should be under an hour
